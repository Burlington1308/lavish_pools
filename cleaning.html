Pool Cleaning

Get rid of leaves and debris, wall stains, algae and bacteria and other contaminants with our cleaning services.

Our services include but are not limited to:

Skim & Brush

We remove debris from the water using a net.

Then we brush the walls, floors and steps to prevent algae.

Vacuum

We remove debris from the floor using a vacuum.

Clean Baskets & Filter

We empty the skimmer and pump baskets, and backwash the filter when pressure rises 10 PSI above normal.

Balance Chemicals

We test the water and check chlorine and pH levels and add chemicals as needed.

Shock the Pool

We shock the water with high doses of chlorine to kill bacteria and algae. This is a periodic treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How Long Do I wait Between Water Shock Treatments?


Non-chlorine shock treatment can be done weekly. Chlorine provides deeper cleaning and can be done at intervals of 2-4weeks.

What is the recommended pH for my pool?


Your water pH should be between 7.4 and 7.6

How do I tell when to backwach my filter?


he most reliable indicator is when the PSI (pounds per square inch) on your filter gauge rises 8–10 PSI above your baseline "clean" pressure.


If the water flowing back into your pool from the return jets feels weak or slow, it means the filter is clogged.


Backwash after heavy rain, storms, or, in some cases, after a pool party with high usage.


If you have just vacuumed a large amount of dirt from the pool floor, a backwash is often necessary.
