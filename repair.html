Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if it’s a leak or just evaporation?


Perform the "bucket test": Place a bucket on a pool step, fill it to match the pool water level, mark both levels, and wait 24–48 hours. If the pool level drops significantly more than the bucket, it is a leak. Normal evaporation is roughly 5mm–10mm per day.

What are the most common causes of pool leaks?


Common causes include cracked underground pipes, faulty plumbing seals, damaged skimmers, worn-out multiport valve gaskets, or cracks in the structure (plaster/vinyl).

How long does a repair take?


Repairs can take from a few hours to a few days, depending on the severity and accessibility of the leak.

Does the pump running affect the leak?


Yes. If the leak stops when the pump is off, it is likely in the pressure-side plumbing. If the leak continues, it is likely in the structure (walls/floor) or suction-side plumbing.
