Pool Resurfacing

Fix leaks, cracks, rough plaster and algae stained surfaces with our premium resurfacing services.

The Resurfacing Process

  • Drain and Inspect: The pool is drained, and the structure is inspected for structural issues, cracks, or leaks.
  • Surface Preparation (Demolition): The old surface is removed via hydro-blasting, sandblasting, or jackhammering. For concrete pools, technicians often use a "sound" test with a hammer to identify and remove delaminated (hollow) plaster.
  • Repairs and Plumbing: Cracks are repaired, and all fittings (lights, jets, drains) are cut back and sealed to ensure a watertight bond.
  • Bond Coat Application: A specialized bonding agent is applied to the old, prepped surface to ensure the new material adheres properly.
  • New Finish Application: The new material—such as plaster, aggregate/pebble, or fiberglass—is applied to the surface.
  • Curing and Acid Wash: The new surface must cure (harden). For pebble finishes, an acid wash is often used to remove residue and expose the aggregate.
  • Refilling and Water Balancing The pool is refilled, and chemicals are balanced during a, startup process that usually lasts one week.


Common Pool Finishes

  • Marbelite/Plaster: Cement-based, traditional, and durable.
  • Exposed Aggregate/Pebble: Highly durable and popular for a modern look.
  • Fiberglass: Long-lasting, smooth, and resistant to algae.
  • Epoxy/Paint: A more budget-friendly option, though generally less durable than others.

Frequently Asked Questions

How Long Does It Take To Resurface a Pool?


The entire process typically takes 5–7 days for smaller jobs, but can last up to 3 weeks for complex, full-remodel projects.

How do I know I need resurfacing?


When you see discoloration, cracks, rough texture, leaking, or exposed concrete you know it's time to get your pool resurfaced.

How long do the materials last?


Plaster lasts 7–10 years, Quartz lasts 10+ years, and Pebble lasts 15+ years.
